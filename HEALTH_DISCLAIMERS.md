ShiftWell does **not** diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent Shift Work Sleep Disorder (SWSD) or any other medical condition. If you experience persistent insomnia, excessive sleepiness, difficulty concentrating, or other symptoms consistent with SWSD, you should seek evaluation from a qualified healthcare provider. Prevalence data (Drake et al., 2013) suggests that a significant proportion of shift workers may meet criteria for SWSD — a recognized clinical diagnosis that requires professional assessment.

### 4.5 No Guarantee of Results

Individual responses to sleep schedule adjustments vary significantly based on chronotype, age, health status, shift schedule, and other factors. ShiftWell makes **no guarantee** that its recommendations will improve your sleep quality, sleep duration, alertness, or overall health. Results described in the scientific literature represent population-level findings and may not reflect any individual user's experience.

You are solely responsible for your own health decisions. By using ShiftWell, you acknowledge that you are voluntarily choosing to follow or disregard the app's recommendations at your own discretion and risk. You should evaluate all recommendations in the context of your personal health status and, where appropriate, in consultation with your healthcare provider.

### 4.7 Shift Worker Safety — Drowsy Driving and Workplace Safety

Shift work is associated with increased risk of fatigue-related accidents, including drowsy driving and workplace injuries. ShiftWell's recommendations are intended to support better sleep habits but **cannot eliminate fatigue risk**. Users must:

- **Never drive or operate heavy machinery if feeling drowsy**, regardless of whether they have followed the app's recommendations.
- Follow all employer safety protocols and workplace fatigue management policies.
- Be aware that transitioning between shift schedules can temporarily increase fatigue, even when following an optimized plan.

If you experience excessive sleepiness while driving or working, stop the activity immediately and seek a safe environment. ShiftWell assumes no responsibility for accidents, injuries, or safety incidents.

### Key Principles

- **Never claim to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent** any disease or condition.
- **Never use "clinically proven"** unless referring to the underlying published research itself (and even then, clarify that the app's specific implementation has not been independently validated).
- **Always include a qualifier** when referencing scientific studies (e.g., "based on," "informed by," "drawing from").
- **Avoid superlatives** like "best," "most effective," or "only" when describing health outcomes.
- **Do reference the science** — ShiftWell's foundation in peer-reviewed research (Borbely, 1982; Eastman & Burgess, 2009; Drake et al., 2013) is a genuine differentiator, as long as claims remain proportionate.
- When in doubt, use language that **empowers the user** ("helps you," "supports," "tools for") rather than language that **promises outcomes** ("fixes," "cures," "guarantees").
