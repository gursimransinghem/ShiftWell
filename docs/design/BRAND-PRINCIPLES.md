# ShiftWell Brand Principles

> The rubric every downstream surface is measured against: paywall copy, App Store listing, website, logo, onboarding, notifications. If a design or line of copy can't defend itself against these seven rules, it doesn't ship.

Audience: 700M shift workers who've been sold every "sleep hack" that didn't work. They've earned skepticism. The brand's job is to be the opposite of the wellness grift — mission-led, citation-led, quietly confident.

---

## The Seven Rules

### 1. Citations visible. Algorithm transparent.
Every scientific claim names its source on the same screen. No "proprietary algorithm," no "our secret sauce," no "AI-powered" when it's a deterministic model. The Two-Process Model is 42 years old — we lean into that, not away from it.

**Example pass:** "Based on Van Dongen et al., 2003 — Walter Reed Army Institute."
**Example fail:** "Powered by ShiftWell's proprietary sleep intelligence engine."

### 2. No urgency theater.
Zero countdown timers, "LAST CHANCE," "Only X spots left," "Price goes up in 24h," or artificially scarce offers — anywhere. Urgency is a manipulation vector; our product is a commitment to the user's long-term biology.

### 3. No fake social proof.
No placeholder testimonials. No invented usernames. No "Join 10,000+ users" until we actually have 10,000 users. Beta quotes only with named, written consent. If we haven't earned the number, we don't display the number.

### 4. Cancellation is louder than conversion.
Every paywall and every App Store description states — in plain language — that cancellation takes one tap from Settings. "No phone calls, no forms." If a user can't find the off-switch in under ten seconds, the design is broken.

### 5. Physician credibility factual, not hyped.
"Built by an emergency medicine physician who works nights" is a biographical fact, and the strongest line we have. We never say "doctor-approved," "clinically endorsed," or "medically validated" — those are regulated claims the product has not earned. Dr. Singh is the founder, not a marketing asset.

### 6. Every hero claim cites a named study with year.
If a headline, landing page hero, or App Store screenshot makes a scientific statement, the citation is visible on the same surface. Accepted anchor studies:
- Borbely, 1982 — Two-Process Model of sleep regulation
- Eastman & Burgess, 2009 — circadian shifting protocols
- NIOSH CDC, 2014/2020 — shift work anchor sleep
- Drake et al., 2004 — Shift Work Sleep Disorder prevalence
- Van Dongen et al., 2003 — cumulative sleep debt
- Chellappa et al., 2021 — daytime eating & CVD in night workers
- AHA Scientific Statement, 2025 — circadian disruption & cardiovascular health
- AASM Guidelines, 2015/2023 — clinical sleep practice

### 7. Mission-first framing.
The product's job is to solve a problem — chronic sleep debt in the people who keep the world running at night. It is not a lifestyle purchase. Hero copy opens with the problem, not the product. Taglines describe what we do for the user, not how we feel about ourselves.

**Example pass:** "Work any shift. Sleep like a champion."
**Example fail:** "Reimagining human performance through science."

## Visual Expression

These follow from the rules, not the other way around.

- **Palette:** dark-mode-first. Night-sky background (#080B14), warm-gold accent (#C8A84B) for earned-not-hyped credibility, circadian-purple (#7B61FF) for the algorithm itself. No gradient-only trust signals.
- **Typography:** system sans (SF Pro / Inter) at weights that read at 3am. Never below 11pt for body copy. Never italicized body.
- **Iconography:** geometric, glyph-style. No photorealistic sleep-tech stock. No "doctor in a white coat" clichés.
- **Motion:** restraint. Subtle (under 200ms). Never looping marketing animations. Motion earns trust when it respects the user's time.
- **Logo:** must read at 20px (app-switcher glyph) and 1024px (App Store icon) without losing its core identity. Symbol must survive monochrome. Wordmark must survive black-on-white and white-on-black.

---

## What This Means Downstream

- **Paywall:** pricing visible before any CTA. Cancel-in-one-tap stated. No trial countdown. Real citations on every stat card.
- **App Store:** no "100% FREE" if it isn't. Opener names the audience, then the problem, then the product.
- **Landing page:** no hero carousel. No testimonial wall until testimonials exist. Pricing page, not a funnel.
- **Onboarding:** no "loading your personalized plan" theater for a deterministic calculation that takes 80ms. Say what happens, then do it.
- **Notifications:** only send when the user's biology needs something. Never for engagement metrics.
