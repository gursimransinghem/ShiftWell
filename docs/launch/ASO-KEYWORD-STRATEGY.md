# ShiftWell — App Store Optimization (ASO) Keyword Strategy

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** April 2026
> **Purpose:** Keyword targeting, screenshot strategy, and A/B testing variants for App Store launch
> **Audience:** Founder, marketing, future ASO consultant

---

## 1. Keyword Strategy Overview

ShiftWell targets a highly specific, underserved audience: shift workers seeking sleep solutions. Broad sleep category keywords are dominated by Calm, Headspace, Rise, and BetterSleep with massive review counts and marketing budgets. ShiftWell wins by dominating the **shift work / night shift / rotating schedule** keyword cluster — terms these competitors have not optimized for.

**ASO positioning:** Own "shift work sleep" the way Rise owns "sleep debt" — be the default answer for the specific search query, not the generic category.

## 2. Primary Keywords (High Priority — Must Appear in Title/Subtitle/Keyword Field)

These 4 terms represent the highest-value search queries with the least competition in the App Store:

| Keyword | Search Intent | Competition Level | Priority |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| shift work sleep | Direct problem statement; highest intent | Low | Critical |
| night shift schedule | Schedule-focused; captures planners | Low | Critical |
| circadian rhythm | Science-aware searcher; premium audience | Medium | Critical |
| sleep optimization | Broader; bridges to general sleep category | Medium | High |

**Implementation:** "Circadian Sleep Plans for Shift Workers" subtitle contains "circadian" and "shift workers" (index-worthy). Title "ShiftWell" + subtitle together cover 3 of 4 primary terms.

## 5. Screenshot Strategy

Apple App Store: 6 screenshots (6.7" iPhone Pro Max format required; other sizes auto-scaled).

### Screenshot Order and Messaging

| # | Screen | Caption | Purpose |
|---|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Onboarding / value prop card | "Built for night shifts. Not for 9-to-5." | Hook — filter for right audience immediately |
| 2 | Today screen with sleep debt + Recovery Score | "Know your sleep debt. Close it tonight." | Core feature — daily actionability |
| 3 | Night Sky visualization | "Your circadian window. Visualized." | Differentiator — no competitor has this |
| 4 | Circadian forecast / week view | "See your week. Own your recovery." | Planning value — shows multi-day intelligence |
| 5 | Weekly Sleep Brief | "Your weekly sleep debrief. In 60 seconds." | Premium feature — aspirational screenshot |
| 6 | Settings / privacy | "All data stays on your device. Always." | Trust — local-first architecture as selling point |

### Screenshot Design Rules

- Dark backgrounds (matches Night Sky brand, filters for night shift identity)
- All screenshots show populated data, not empty states
- Device frame included (iPhone 15 Pro)
- Caption text: 24pt minimum, high contrast, left-aligned
- No more than 6 words per caption
- Screenshots 1–3 tell the story without reading captions (visual-first)

### Alternative A/B Test Variants (Screenshot 1)

| Variant | Caption | Hypothesis |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| A (control) | "Built for night shifts. Not for 9-to-5." | Identity-based hook |
| B | "Import your shifts. Get your sleep plan." | Action-based hook |
| C | "Used by ER doctors. Built by one." | Credibility hook |

Test duration: minimum 2 weeks, minimum 500 impressions per variant before concluding.

## 7. App Store Connect Category Strategy

**Primary category:** Health & Fitness
**Secondary category:** Medical (set after initial reviews accumulate — "Medical" improves credibility for enterprise sales conversations but may reduce casual discovery volume)

**Subcategory positioning:**
- Do NOT select "Sleep" subcategory — it puts ShiftWell directly against Calm, Headspace, Rise at disadvantage
- Health & Fitness > "Fitness" positioning is more favorable for initial ranking

## 8. Review Strategy

Target review volume at launch:
- Week 1: 10+ reviews (from beta testers, personal network)
- Month 1: 50+ reviews (from TestFlight alumni)
- Month 3: 200+ reviews (organic + in-app prompt at optimal moment)

**In-app review prompt timing:**
- Trigger after user completes their first full sleep plan (high positive affect moment)
- Never prompt immediately after onboarding (user hasn't experienced value yet)
- Use SKStoreReviewAPI (never redirect to App Store — violates guidelines)

**Review velocity matters:** Apple's algorithm weights recent reviews. Aim for consistent weekly reviews rather than a large batch at launch.
