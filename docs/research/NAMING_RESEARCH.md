## Critical Finding: "ShiftWell" Has Conflicts

**The name "ShiftWell" is already taken.** BC Emergency Health Services (BCEHS) has an app called "ShiftWell" on both the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/ca/app/shiftwell/id1527644853) and [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=ca.bcehs.shiftwell&gl=US). It targets shift workers (first responders) with schedule management, self-care goals, and wellness resources. This is a direct competitor in the same space.

Additionally:
- **shiftwell.io** -- shift scheduling platform (active)
- **shiftwell.ai** -- AI coaching/behavioral science platform (active, hosted on Odoo)
- **shiftwell.co** -- registered, appears active
- **shift-well.com** -- registered
- **shiftwell.com** -- likely registered (not confirmed available)

**Verdict: ShiftWell should be renamed.** Trademark conflict is near-certain in Class 9 (downloadable software) and Class 10/44 (health/medical). The BCEHS app is in the exact same category (shift worker wellness). Proceeding with this name would invite a cease-and-desist.

### Naming Patterns That Win

1. **Short (1-2 syllables, 4-5 letters)**: Rise, Calm, Whoop, Oura, Noom, Peaks -- easy to say, type, search
2. **Aspirational/outcome-focused**: Rise (you go up), Calm (you become calm), Peaks (you hit peaks)
3. **Science-adjacent but not clinical**: Timeshifter, Circadian -- nod to science without being a textbook
4. **Made-up but phonetically pleasing**: Oura, Noom, Whoop -- unique, defensible trademarks
5. **Emotional resonance over description**: "Calm" beats "MeditationHelper"; "Rise" beats "SleepDebtTracker"

### 2025-2026 Wellness Branding Trends

- Moving from functional/goal-driven to **emotional and ritualistic** language (ritual, soul, heal)
- **Hyper-specificity** wins over broad wellness claims -- name the job, not the category
- **Trust and credibility** matter more than cleverness -- science-backed positioning
- **Premium minimalism** in naming -- fewer syllables, cleaner typography
- Consumers respond to names that feel like **a verb or state of being** (Rise, Calm, Flow)

## Top 5 Recommendations (Ranked)

### 1. Sova
- **Why**: Short (4 letters), warm, unique. Means "owl" in several Slavic languages -- a nocturnal creature that thrives at night. Perfect brand story for shift workers AND broader audience. Nobody owns this in the health app space.
- **Domain**: sova.app likely available; sova.com needs checking
- **Expansion**: Works for sleep, circadian, wellness -- no ceiling
- **Risk**: Low trademark risk in health/wellness

### 2. Circadex
- **Why**: Immediately signals circadian science. The "-dex" suffix (like index, Pokedex) implies a smart system/database. Unique, defensible, premium.
- **Domain**: circadex.com likely available; circadex.app likely available
- **Expansion**: Broad -- circadian rhythm affects everyone
- **Risk**: Very low -- novel compound word

### 3. Nox
- **Why**: Latin for "night." Three letters. Incredibly brandable. Works as a sleep app name, works as a broader wellness brand. Think: "Nox knows your night."
- **Domain**: nox.app likely available; nox.com likely taken but nox.health or nox.io possible
- **Expansion**: Broad but weighted toward sleep/night
- **Risk**: Low-medium -- check for Nox in gaming/other categories

### 4. Entrain
- **Why**: The actual scientific term for synchronizing circadian rhythms to external cues (like light). It IS what the app does. Credibility with healthcare professionals baked into the name.
- **Domain**: entrain.app likely available; entrain.com needs checking
- **Expansion**: Broad -- entrainment applies to everyone
- **Risk**: Medium -- real English word, check existing uses. MIT had a jet lag app called "Entrain" (may be defunct)

### 5. Vesper
- **Why**: Means "evening" or "evening star" in Latin. Beautiful, premium, evocative. Think: "Your evening, optimized." Works for the aspirational/luxury wellness market.
- **Domain**: vesper.app possibly available; vesper.com likely taken
- **Expansion**: Very broad -- premium wellness brand
- **Risk**: Medium -- Vesper was a now-defunct note-taking app; James Bond character
