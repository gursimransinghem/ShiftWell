# Rise Science (RISE Sleep)

## 1. Founding Story

**Founders:** Jeff Kahn (CEO) and Leon Sasson (CTO), with Jacob Kelter as a co-founder.

**When:** Founded 2014, consumer app launched mid-2020.

**Origin:** Jeff Kahn was a sophomore at Northwestern University studying engineering when he became obsessed with sleep science. He customized his curriculum to pursue combined studies in health systems engineering and set up independent studies with sleep scientists at the Feinberg School of Medicine. He met Leon Sasson, a computer science and industrial engineering student. Together, they started exploring how sleep optimization could improve human performance.

**Original Insight:** Sleep debt (cumulative sleep loss) is the single most actionable metric for energy and performance. Not sleep quality scores, not "stages" -- just the raw hours you owe your body. This was radical simplicity vs. the "sleep score" approach of Fitbit/Oura.

**Early Work (2014-2019):** Before the consumer app, Rise Science worked exclusively with professional sports teams (NFL, MLB, NBA, MLS, NCAAF) and Fortune 500 sales teams. They perfected their tools in high-performance environments where ROI was directly measurable. A study with Northwestern's Kellogg Sales Institute showed sales teams using RISE drove a **14% increase in revenue** and **50% increase in outbound efforts**.

**Consumer Pivot (2018-2020):** Kahn's "aha" moment came in 2018 when he realized sleep-driven performance gains weren't just for pro athletes. The consumer app launched in mid-2020 -- perfect pandemic timing.

Jeff and Leon were named **Forbes 30 Under 30**.

## 7. Paywall Strategy

**Type:** Hard paywall with free trial.

**How it works:**
1. Long, engaging onboarding quiz (see #8)
2. Scientific pop-ups during quiz build authority
3. Personalized results shown (sleep debt calculated)
4. Single primary offer: 7-day free trial → $69.99/year
5. No free tier -- you must subscribe to use the app

**Why this works:**
- Users invest 5-10 minutes in the quiz, creating sunk cost / commitment bias
- The quiz reveals a "problem" (your sleep debt number) that only the app can solve
- The free trial is long enough (7 days) to demonstrate "80% of users feel more energy within 5 days"
- Annual pricing ($0.19/day) feels trivially cheap after seeing the problem

**Lesson for ShiftWell:** Hard paywall + long quiz + personalized "diagnosis" = high conversion. The quiz IS the product demo.

## 8. Onboarding Flow

**Goals of onboarding (2 main):**
1. Convince people that sleep matters more than they think
2. Convince people that RISE is the app to fix their sleep problem

**What they ask:**

- Small pop-ups during questions provide scientific context ("Did you know: 1 in 3 adults don't get enough sleep")

- Results screen shows personalized sleep debt number + circadian rhythm prediction

**Duration:** ~5-10 minutes. Deliberately long. They're optimizing for commitment, not speed.

## 10. What Makes Their Website BAD

- **Desperation for subscribers is visible:** The entire site is a conversion funnel. Every page leads to "download the app." There's no genuinely free value -- it's all gated.
- **Excessively long landing pages:** Hero sections are followed by scroll after scroll of testimonials, feature blocks, and social proof. It feels like an infomercial.
- **Aggressive ad retargeting:** RISE shipped **620 fresh ads in one month** and runs 85% UGC-style ads. Users report feeling "followed" by Rise ads across platforms.
- **Free trial dark patterns:** Some users report being charged after cancelling during trial. Deleting the account does NOT cancel the subscription -- you must cancel through Apple/Google separately.
- **Pricing opacity:** Hard to find exact pricing before starting trial. The paywall inside the app shows pricing, but the website deliberately avoids mentioning cost.
- **No free tier exists:** Unlike competitors (Sleep Cycle has a free tier), Rise offers zero functionality without payment. This generates negative reviews from cost-sensitive users.

## 12. Key Features That Drive Retention

1. **Sleep Debt number:** A single, trackable metric that creates daily engagement. Users check it like a bank balance.
2. **Energy Schedule (circadian rhythm prediction):** Tells you when you'll peak and dip throughout the day. Makes the app useful beyond bedtime.
3. **20+ smart sleep habit reminders:** Timed to your personal circadian rhythm (e.g., "Last coffee by 2pm," "Start winding down at 9:30pm").
4. **Wearable integrations:** Apple Watch, Fitbit, Oura Ring, Sleep Cycle -- reduces friction for existing tracker users.
5. **The "payback" loop:** Seeing sleep debt go down over days/weeks creates a gamification effect without badges or streaks.
6. **Guided relaxation exercises:** Breathing and relaxation exercises for bedtime.

**Why it works:** The sleep debt number creates a "gap" (you owe X hours). The app provides a path to close it. This is classic habit loop: cue (notification), routine (follow recommendation), reward (debt decreases).

## 13. What Users Complain About Most

**From Trustpilot, Reddit, App Store reviews, and review sites:**

1. **Billing/subscription issues:** Charged after cancelling trial. No monthly/weekly cheap option. $60-70/year feels expensive for "just a sleep tracker."
2. **Sleep debt accuracy:** Core calculation is "wrong half the time" for some users. Limited options to fix errors beyond contacting support.
3. **Can't delete bad data:** Users cannot delete erroneously recorded sleep periods, only edit length. If the phone records "sleep" when you were watching TV, it corrupts your data.
4. **Unrealistic recommendations:** App suggests sleep/wake times that don't match real-life constraints (e.g., shift workers, parents with young kids).
5. **No free tier:** Nothing useful without paying. Generates 1-star reviews from people who downloaded expecting basic functionality.
6. **Notification overload:** Some find the 20+ habit reminders excessive.
7. **Price vs. value:** The app doesn't do "enough" compared to free alternatives for casual users.

**Critical insight for ShiftWell:** Rise's biggest weakness is that it doesn't understand non-standard schedules. Shift workers -- our exact audience -- are underserved by Rise's circadian rhythm model which assumes a regular 9-5 schedule.

Sources: [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/risescience.com), [JustUseApp](https://justuseapp.com/en/app/1453884781/rise-sleep-energy-tracker/reviews), [Google Play Reviews](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.risesci.nyx&hl=en_US)

# Calm

## 1. Founding Story

**Founders:** Michael Acton Smith and Alex Tew

**When:** Founded May 4, 2012

**The Pivot from calm.com:**
- In 2011, Alex Tew created "Do Nothing for 2 Minutes" -- a web experience that simply showed an ocean scene with a timer. If you moved your mouse, the timer reset. It went viral, proving massive latent demand for digital mindfulness.
- Alex Tew was already famous for the "Million Dollar Homepage" (2005), where he sold pixels to finance his university education.
- Michael Acton Smith had founded Firebox.com (1998) and Moshi Monsters (a kids' virtual world that hit 80M users and $200M valuation). After Moshi Monsters stalled due to the mobile shift, he burned out and began exploring meditation.
- Smith and Tew, both living in Soho, London, regularly hung out playing video games and brainstorming. During one session, they conceived Calm.
- They launched calm.com as a simple web experience, then pivoted to a full meditation app.

**Early struggle:** It took nearly six months and meetings with 100+ potential investors before Calm secured its first angel investors. Nobody wanted to fund a "mental health app" in 2012. They raised just $1.5M between 2012 and 2014. Smith used money he'd saved to buy a house.

## 6. How Calm Became an Employee Benefit Standard

### The Playbook

1. **Build consumer brand first.** Calm spent 2012-2018 building massive consumer awareness. By the time they pitched HR departments, employees were already asking for it.

2. **Celebrity partnerships as brand moat.** Matthew McConaughey, LeBron James, Harry Styles, Michael Buble -- celebrity sleep stories made Calm a household name. LeBron was using the app daily and became an investor. This wasn't just endorsement; it was authentic usage.

3. **Ride the mental health wave.** COVID made mental health a C-suite priority overnight. Calm was already positioned as the #1 mental health app. HR teams needed to "do something" about employee burnout -- Calm was the easy, brandable answer.

4. **Build the clinical product separately.** Calm Health (launched 2022) is a distinct, evidence-based app for healthcare/employer distribution. This gave Calm two products: the consumer brand (acquisition) and the clinical tool (enterprise).

5. **Insurance integration as distribution.** Partnering with UnitedHealthcare and Optum gave Calm access to 50M+ Americans without selling to each employer individually. The insurer does the distribution.

6. **ROI data for the pitch.** Calm provides employers with:
   - Analytics dashboards showing engagement
   - Benchmarking tools
   - Data on absenteeism reduction, healthcare cost savings
   - The headline stat: burnout-related turnover costs up to 20% of total payroll; poor wellbeing costs $20M per 10,000 employees

7. **Make it easy to buy.** Calm Business integrates with existing benefits platforms, Slack, Teams. IT doesn't need to build anything. HR can launch it in a week.

### Why This Matters for ShiftWell

Calm's B2B playbook took **7+ years** of consumer brand-building before enterprise became viable. ShiftWell can shortcut this by:
- Starting with a clinical/regulatory angle (OSHA, Joint Commission) instead of brand awareness
- Targeting a specific employer vertical (healthcare systems, EMS agencies) instead of "all companies"
- Using physician credibility instead of celebrity endorsements
- Positioning as compliance/risk management, not "wellness"

## 10. Calm's Moat

1. **Brand recognition:** "Calm" is synonymous with meditation apps for most consumers. First-mover advantage in the category.
2. **Content library:** 700+ hours of original content, celebrity Sleep Stories (1B+ plays). Expensive and time-consuming to replicate.
3. **Enterprise distribution:** 4,000-5,000 companies + insurance partnerships covering 50M Americans. This took years to build.
4. **Data flywheel:** Millions of users generating behavioral data that improves recommendations.
5. **Celebrity relationships:** Not just endorsements -- LeBron is an investor, Harry Styles created content. These are deep, authentic connections.
6. **Switching costs:** Employers who've rolled out Calm to 50,000 employees don't switch easily. The integration, training, and reporting are sticky.

**What's NOT a moat:** The meditation content itself. Any startup can produce guided meditations. The moat is brand + distribution + enterprise integration.

## 11. Weaknesses

1. **Subscriber decline:** 5M peak → 3.5M in 2025. That's a 30% loss in 3 years. The consumer product is leaking.
2. **Revenue decline:** From $355M+ to possibly $210M in 2025. The business may be shrinking.
3. **Layoffs:** 20% workforce reduction in August 2022 (~90 people). Employee reviews mention "three layoffs in three years."
4. **Internal morale:** Glassdoor reviews describe "consistent restructuring of KPIs and quotas killing the sales team" and "very low morale among AEs."
5. **Post-pandemic normalization:** The mental health app boom is cooling. Users who downloaded Calm in 2020 aren't renewing.
6. **Content fatigue:** Meditation content has a ceiling. Users report getting bored and churning.
7. **Generic positioning:** "Mental wellness for everyone" is broad but shallow. No deep expertise in any specific domain.
8. **Regulatory gap:** Calm isn't a medical device or clinical tool (consumer app). Calm Health is the clinical bet, but it's still early.

Sources: [MobiHealthNews](https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/meditation-app-calm-lays-20-workforce), [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/Calm-layoff-Reviews-EI_IE1697524.0,4_KH5,11.htm)

## 12. Lessons for ShiftWell from Calm's Enterprise Playbook

1. **Build consumer credibility before selling B2B.** Employees request tools they already use. Get shift workers using ShiftWell individually first.
2. **Create a separate clinical product for enterprise.** Calm Health is distinct from consumer Calm. ShiftWell should have a "ShiftWell for Organizations" with analytics, compliance reporting, and admin dashboards.
3. **Insurance distribution is the unlock.** Calm's UHC partnership reaches 50M Americans. ShiftWell should pursue health plan partnerships, especially workers' comp insurers who cover shift workers.
4. **ROI data is the enterprise sales tool.** Build case studies showing: fewer fatigue-related incidents, lower workers' comp claims, reduced turnover, OSHA compliance.
5. **Make it trivially easy for HR to deploy.** SSO integration, benefits platform compatibility, zero IT work needed.
6. **Occupational specificity is a wedge.** Calm Health has nurse-specific and teacher-specific content. ShiftWell IS occupational-specific. That's the entire product.
7. **Don't compete on content volume.** Calm has 700+ hours. ShiftWell should compete on specificity and clinical outcomes, not library size.

# Enterprise Adoption Playbook {#enterprise-adoption-playbook}

Based on Calm's success getting into employee benefits, adapted for ShiftWell's healthcare/shift worker vertical:

## 1. Typical Sales Cycle

**Calm's experience:**
- Small companies (under 500 employees): 1-3 months
- Mid-market (500-5,000): 3-6 months
- Enterprise (5,000+): 6-12 months
- Health plan integration: 12-18 months (regulatory, compliance, contracting)

**ShiftWell's likely cycle:**
- Individual hospital departments: 1-3 months (champion-driven)
- Health system-wide: 6-12 months (requires CNO/CMO buy-in + compliance review)
- EMS agencies: 1-3 months (smaller, faster decisions)
- Workers' comp insurers: 12-18 months (actuarial review, outcomes data needed)

## 2. Who's the Buyer?

**Calm's buyers:**
- HR / People Operations (primary)
- Benefits team (budget holder)
- C-suite sponsor (CEO/CHRO for large deals)

**ShiftWell's buyers (different and more favorable):**
- **Chief Nursing Officer (CNO):** Owns nurse fatigue, scheduling, retention
- **Risk Management / Patient Safety:** Fatigue-related errors are a liability issue
- **Occupational Health:** Workers' comp cost reduction
- **HR / Benefits:** Standard wellness benefit channel
- **EMS Medical Director:** Clinical authority for agency-wide adoption
- **Fire Chief / Police Chief:** Operational readiness angle

**Key advantage:** ShiftWell has multiple buyer personas within the same organization. Calm has one (HR). ShiftWell can enter through risk management OR nursing leadership OR occupational health -- whichever door opens first.

## 3. What ROI Data Do Employers Need?

**What Calm provides:**
- Engagement rates and usage analytics
- Self-reported wellbeing improvements
- Benchmarking vs. industry averages
- General claims: "burnout costs $322B annually"

**What ShiftWell needs to provide (and can):**
- **Fatigue-related incident reduction:** Track pre/post implementation incident rates
- **Workers' comp claims data:** Fewer injuries during overnight shifts
- **Nurse turnover reduction:** #1 reason nurses leave is burnout/scheduling; show retention improvement
- **OSHA compliance documentation:** Automated fatigue risk management reporting
- **Joint Commission readiness:** Fatigue management is a surveyed element
- **Absenteeism reduction:** Shift workers miss more work due to health issues
- **Patient safety metrics:** Fewer medication errors during fatigue-prone hours

**The ShiftWell ROI pitch is fundamentally stronger than Calm's** because it ties to measurable, hard-dollar outcomes (workers' comp, turnover, incident reduction) rather than soft wellness metrics.

## 4. Compliance/Regulatory Angle ShiftWell Has That Calm Doesn't

This is ShiftWell's single biggest enterprise advantage:

| Regulation/Standard | What It Requires | How ShiftWell Helps | Calm's Answer |
|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| **OSHA General Duty Clause** | Employers must provide safe workplace; fatigue is a recognized hazard | Automated fatigue risk assessment, shift-specific recommendations | Nothing specific |
| **Joint Commission Fatigue Management** | Hospitals must have fatigue management plans | Turnkey compliance tool: tracks, documents, reports | Nothing specific |
| **ACGME Duty Hours** | Resident work hour limits | Monitoring and alerting for medical trainees | Nothing specific |
| **NFPA 1584** | Fire service fatigue management | Firefighter-specific fatigue assessment | Nothing specific |
| **DOT Hours of Service** | Driver fatigue prevention | Not directly applicable but adjacent | Nothing specific |
| **State Nurse Staffing Laws** | Various states mandate fatigue considerations | Documentation and compliance reporting | Nothing specific |

**Calm sells "wellness." ShiftWell can sell "compliance."** Compliance is a must-buy. Wellness is a nice-to-have. This completely changes the sales conversation.

## Strategic Recommendations for ShiftWell

### From Rise, we learn:
1. **One metric simplicity works.** Rise's sleep debt number is the app. ShiftWell needs its own single, compelling metric (e.g., "Shift Readiness Score" or "Recovery Debt").
2. **Hard paywall + long quiz = high conversion.** Copy Rise's onboarding pattern: educational quiz → personalized result → paywall with free trial.
3. **Science credibility converts.** Rise's Northwestern/peer-reviewed positioning builds trust. ShiftWell's physician-built angle is even stronger.
4. **You don't need $200M.** Rise built a fast-growing app on $15.5M. Capital efficiency matters.
5. **Rise ignores shift workers.** Their circadian model assumes regular schedules. This is our gap to fill.

### From Calm, we learn:
1. **Enterprise is the long game.** Calm's B2B took 7+ years to build. Start laying groundwork now, even if revenue comes later.
2. **Build a separate enterprise product.** Calm Health is distinct from consumer Calm. ShiftWell should plan for "ShiftWell for Organizations" from day one.
3. **Insurance distribution is the multiplier.** One deal with a workers' comp insurer or UHC-equivalent can reach millions of shift workers.
4. **Compliance sells better than wellness.** Calm sells "employee wellness." ShiftWell can sell "fatigue risk management compliance." One is discretionary spending; the other is legally required.
5. **Content is a moat, but not the only moat.** Calm's 700+ hours of content is expensive. ShiftWell's moat should be data/algorithm + clinical credibility + regulatory compliance, not content.
6. **Subscriber churn is real.** Calm lost 30% of paid users in 3 years. ShiftWell needs retention mechanics beyond content (shift schedule integration, daily utility like Rise's energy timeline).
