### Market Size
- Global circadian rhythm sleep treatment market: **$2.4B** (2025), projected **$3.7B** by 2032 (CAGR 6.5%)
- Sleep tracking & optimization products: **$3.7B** (2025), projected **$20.8B** by 2035 (CAGR 19%)
- Shift Work Disorder prevalence: **26.5%** of all shift workers; **32-44%** of nurses

### Key Gaps in the Market
1. **No app combines shift scheduling + circadian optimization + social coordination** in one product
2. **No calendar-sync auto-scheduling** exists — every app requires manual shift entry
3. **No "household mode"** coordinating sleep with partners/family
4. **No commute-aware wake times** or dynamic rescheduling based on real-world logistics
5. **No Live Activities / Dynamic Island** integration for sleep countdowns
6. **AI weekly check-ins** are absent from every competitor
7. **Night Sky Mode** (dark astronomy-inspired UI) does not exist anywhere
8. **No routine builder** that handles AM/PM logistics (meal prep, caffeine timing, light exposure, exercise) as an integrated workflow

## 7. Gap Analysis — What NOBODY Does

### 7.1 Calendar Sync + Auto-Scheduling
**Gap:** Every shift work app requires manual entry of shifts. No app reads from Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, QGenda, or hospital scheduling systems to automatically detect shift patterns and generate sleep plans.

**Our opportunity:** Automatic calendar import + pattern detection + instant sleep plan generation. One-tap setup from existing schedule.

### 7.2 AI-Powered Weekly Check-Ins
**Gap:** No competitor offers conversational AI check-ins that review the past week's adherence, identify patterns, adjust recommendations, and provide motivation.

**Our opportunity:** Weekly "How's it going?" conversational AI that adapts the plan based on what actually happened vs. what was recommended.

### 7.3 Shift Crew (Social Coordination)
**Gap:** Zero competitors offer social features for co-workers on the same shift pattern. No shared accountability, no group challenges, no crew-based optimization.

**Our opportunity:** Crew formation for same-shift coworkers. Shared goals, leaderboards, accountability partners, coordinated light exposure/caffeine timing.

### 7.4 Night Sky Mode
**Gap:** No sleep app offers a truly dark, astronomy-inspired UI designed for use in dark environments without disrupting night vision or melatonin production.

**Our opportunity:** Purpose-built dark UI with red-shifted colors, OLED-optimized true blacks, minimal blue light emission. A UI that feels like it was designed for the night.

### 7.5 Routine Builder (AM/PM Logistics)
**Gap:** Existing apps tell you "sleep at 10pm" or "avoid caffeine after 2pm" but don't help you build the full routine around it — meal prep timing, exercise windows, light box sessions, medication schedules, winding-down rituals.

**Our opportunity:** Full daily routine templates that adapt to each shift type. "Here's your entire day planned, not just your bedtime."

### 7.6 Live Activities / Dynamic Island
**Gap:** No sleep or shift work app uses iOS Live Activities to show countdown to next sleep window, current circadian phase, or caffeine cutoff time.

**Our opportunity:** Persistent glanceable info on lock screen and Dynamic Island — "Sleep in 2h 14m" or "Caffeine cutoff: NOW".

### 7.7 Dynamic Rescheduling
**Gap:** All competitors create static plans. If you miss a sleep window, oversleep, or pick up an extra shift, the plan doesn't adapt.

**Our opportunity:** Real-time plan adaptation. Missed your nap? The plan recalculates. Picked up an overtime shift? New sleep windows generated automatically.

### 7.8 Commute-Aware Wake Times
**Gap:** No app accounts for commute duration when calculating wake times. A 45-minute commute means you need to wake up earlier, which changes the entire sleep plan.

**Our opportunity:** Integrate commute time (manual or Maps API) into wake time calculations. "Your shift starts at 7pm and your commute is 40 minutes — wake at 5:30pm."

### 7.9 Household Mode
**Gap:** Zero competitors address the reality that shift workers live with partners and families who have different schedules. No app helps coordinate quiet hours, shared meals, or bedroom use.

**Our opportunity:** Multi-user household scheduling. "Your partner is sleeping 11pm-7am and you need to sleep 8am-4pm — here's how to coordinate your shared space."

### 7.10 Borbely Two-Process Model Integration
**Gap:** While Fatigue Science uses SAFTE and Arcashift uses circadian simulation, no consumer app transparently implements and explains the two-process model (Process S homeostatic + Process C circadian) as the foundation of its recommendations.

**Our opportunity:** Use the Borbely model (already ported in our codebase) as the scientific backbone, with clear visual explanations so users understand *why* the recommendations work.
