## The Mission

**Give the world back its time and health.**

It's not just night shift workers. It's the parent working 7-4 who coaches soccer at 5, makes dinner at 6, helps with homework until 9, and scrolls their phone until midnight because it's the only "me time" they get. It's the student juggling classes and a part-time job. It's the 9-to-5er whose kids, fatigue, responsibilities, and life quietly derail their sleep without them even noticing.

700 million people work shifts — but billions more have hectic, unpredictable lives that make consistent sleep feel impossible. Anyone can get off track. Everyone deserves a system that keeps them on it.

ShiftWell exists to automate the one thing everyone needs and nobody prioritizes: rest. Personalized to your daily choices. Adapted to your real life. Consistent when everything else isn't.

Whether you're a nurse rotating through nights, a parent barely keeping up with Monday through Friday, or a founder running on caffeine and ambition — your schedule is met with an intelligent system that handles sleep for you. ## Core Principles

### 1. Automation Over Effort

The user should never have to think. Not about when to sleep, not about when to stop drinking coffee, not about what time to set an alarm. The app thinks. The calendar reflects. The user lives.

*"If the user has to make a decision about sleep, we failed."*

### 2. Science Over Opinion

Every recommendation traces to published, peer-reviewed research. Not wellness blogs. Not influencer advice. Not vibes. The Two-Process Model (Borbely, 1982). AASM Clinical Practice Guidelines. Eastman & Burgess circadian protocols. Drake caffeine studies. AHA cardiovascular statements.

*"Built by a physician who reads the papers and lives the problem."*

### 3. Premium Over Desperate

The product speaks for itself. No pop-ups. No countdown timers. No "LAST CHANCE" banners. No sneaky auto-renewals. No buried cancellation flows. No guilt trips. No dark patterns. Ever.

When someone opens ShiftWell, they should feel respected — not sold to. The experience should be so good that paying for it feels like a privilege, not a trap.

*"If we have to trick someone into subscribing, the product isn't good enough."*

### 4. Invisible Over Impressive

The best features are the ones you never notice working. Sleep blocks appear in your calendar. Your alarm adjusts because tomorrow is free. Your phone goes quiet at bedtime. Your partner gets a notification to keep it down.

None of this requires opening the app. The engine runs invisibly. The app is just the reward — the dashboard, the insights, the proof that it's working.

*"The best sleep app is the one you never have to open."*

### 5. Mission Over Money

Revenue is necessary. Revenue funds the mission. But the mission is not revenue. The mission is giving people their nights back. If a feature makes money but hurts the user experience, we don't build it. If a free tier change increases conversions but feels manipulative, we don't ship it.

*"We'd rather have 10,000 users who love us than 100,000 who tolerate us."*

## The Lightbulb Moments

These are the ideas that emerged during the founding vision session — the ones that made us stop and say "nobody does this." Each one is a reason ShiftWell exists in its own category.

### The Calendar IS the Interface

**The insight:** People don't live in apps. They live in their calendar. If ShiftWell puts sleep blocks directly into Apple Calendar or Google Calendar as real, color-coded events — sleep becomes as non-negotiable as a work shift. It's scheduled. It's visible. It's real.

**Why it matters:** Every competitor makes you open their app to see your plan. We put the plan where you already look. The app is the brain. The calendar is the life.

**Nobody does this.** Timeshifter requires manual entry. Arcashift is buggy. Rise doesn't even try. We read your calendar AND write back to it. Automatically. Dynamically.

---

### Night Sky Mode

**The insight:** The app should know what time it is and transform itself. As bedtime approaches, the UI shifts to a deep night sky — soft firefly animations, minimal text, only the information that matters right now: alarm set, latest wake time, tomorrow's first obligation, projected sleep quality.

**Why it matters:** Most apps look the same at 2 PM and 2 AM. ShiftWell becomes a different experience at night — calming, soothing, almost meditative. Opening the app at bedtime should feel like the beginning of your wind-down, not another screen demanding attention.

**The recharge animation:** A visual energy meter shows your projected sleep quality. If you're still awake past your suggested bedtime, the meter drops in real-time. Not as punishment — as honest, gentle awareness. "You're now looking at 5.8h instead of 7.2h."

---

### The Weekly Check-In (AI That Learns You)

**The insight:** No algorithm can know that you have a stressful presentation Wednesday, your kid's soccer game Saturday morning, or that you've been stress-eating at midnight. But a 90-second weekly conversation can.

**How it works:** Five quick-tap questions (prewritten, fun, engaging) plus 1-3 dynamic AI-generated follow-ups based on YOUR data. "I noticed you slept 2 hours less on Wednesdays the last 3 weeks — what happens on Tuesdays?" The AI gets smarter every week because you're feeding it context no sensor can capture.

**Why it matters:** This turns a sleep app into a sleep partner. Over time, it knows you better than you know yourself. And it costs us ~$0.40/user/month to run on Claude API — 8% of premium revenue. The AI is the moat.

---

### Shift Crew — "Spread the Sleep"

**The insight:** Shift workers are isolated. Their free time doesn't overlap with normal people. Coordinating plans with coworkers who share the same crazy schedule is nearly impossible. But their coworkers are the only people who truly understand.

**How it works:** You invite your crew for a shared shift rotation. The AI scans everyone's schedules, finds overlapping free time, and suggests: "You and Sam both have 40 min free at 5:30 PM — coffee at Blue Bottle, 0.4 mi from the hospital?" Both thumbs up → calendar event created with address and directions. Raincheck button if plans change (soft, not a hard decline).

**Why it's game-changing:**
- It's a natural viral loop — every invite brings a new user from the exact target audience
- It solves a real non-sleep problem (social isolation) using the same schedule data
- It's temporary by design — connections last for the shift block, then fade. No social graph. No feed. No noise.
- Nobody in any app category does this

---

### The Routine Builder (Your Life, Mapped)

**The insight:** Sleep doesn't exist in a vacuum. If you need 45 minutes to get ready, 25 minutes to commute, and you like a 15-minute buffer — and your shift starts at 7 AM — your alarm needs to be at 5:30 AM. Work backward: 7 hours of sleep means bedtime at 10:30 PM. Wind-down notification at 10:00 PM. Caffeine cutoff at 2:00 PM.

No sleep app asks about your shower time. Or your commute. Or your kids' breakfast routine. Or your home and work addresses. ShiftWell does. Because sleep optimization without life logistics is just math homework.

**The commute-aware wake time:** Pull local traffic data + work address → "Leave by 6:12 AM — 23 min drive, light traffic." Nobody does this.

**The free morning detection:** No shift tomorrow AND no calendar events until noon? The alarm disappears. Sleep in. Your body decides when you've had enough. Nobody does this either.

### The Daily Communication Rhythm

**The insight:** The app shouldn't speak to you once. It should accompany you through the day — gently, warmly, at the right moments.

| Time | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| **Wake-up** | Push: "Good morning 🌅 You got 6.8h. You have 2 free hours before your shift — grab a walk?" |
| **30-45 min later** | Detailed push: sleep score, energy prediction, schedule, caffeine cutoff, recommended bedtime + why |
| **Mid-day** | "☕ Last call for coffee in 30 min" |
| **Evening** | "🌙 Wind-down starts in 20 min" |
| **Bedtime** | Live Activity on lock screen: "Sleep 😴" or "It can wait until tomorrow" |
| **Morning prompt** | "Got anything on your mind? Brain dump it here →" (deep-link to quick capture) |

**The tone:** Warm. Emoji. Minimal. Like a friend who happens to be a sleep scientist. Never clinical. Never nagging.

### The Enterprise Play — Compliance Over Wellness

**The insight:** Calm spent 7 years building a consumer brand before selling to enterprises as a "wellness benefit." That's optional. HR can cut it anytime.

ShiftWell sells **compliance**. "Are you meeting OSHA fatigue management guidelines for your night shift staff?" That's not optional. That's a legal department question. That's a risk management question. That's a "how much is your malpractice insurance going up?" question.

**The pitch that closes deals:** "How many nurses did you lose last month to a call-out the night before? How much does one fatigue-related medication error cost in malpractice? ShiftWell costs less than a single agency nurse shift."

**The insurance multiplier:** Workers' comp insurers promote ShiftWell to employers to reduce claims. Every hospital, airline, trucking company gets it through their insurer. We don't sell to them — the insurer distributes for us.

## The Longer Story (For the Website, Investors, Press)

I'm an emergency medicine physician. I work days, nights, evenings, and everything in between. For years, I tried to figure out when to sleep around my shifts — Googling "how to recover from night shift," setting random alarms, drinking coffee at the wrong time, lying in bed at 8 AM with the sun blasting through the curtains, wondering why I can't fall asleep even though I've been awake for 22 hours.

I realized the science exists. The Two-Process Model has been published since 1982. Circadian shifting protocols have been validated in clinical trials. Caffeine half-life calculations are straightforward math. The research is all there.

But nobody connected it to my actual calendar.

So I built ShiftWell. It reads my schedule, generates a complete plan — sleep windows, naps, caffeine cutoffs, meal timing, light protocols — and puts it all directly into my calendar. I don't think about sleep anymore. I just follow the plan.

Now I want every shift worker in the world to have the same thing.
