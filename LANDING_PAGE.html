The Science

Every recommendation traces
to published research

ShiftWell doesn't generate advice. It applies specific mechanisms from peer-reviewed sleep science, deterministically.

Paper 01
Two-Process Model of Sleep Regulation
Borbely, 1982 · Sleep
Sleep timing is determined by two interacting processes: homeostatic sleep pressure (Process S) that builds during waking and dissipates during sleep, and the circadian rhythm (Process C) that modulates alertness independent of recent sleep.
ShiftWell uses this for
Sleep window timing engine — calculating optimal sleep onset/offset for any shift pattern
Paper 02
Circadian Phase Shifting for Night Work
Eastman & Burgess, 2009 · JBMR
Light exposure timing and intensity can shift the circadian clock by up to 2 hours per day. Strategic anchor sleep windows combined with phased light exposure protocols enable controlled phase shifting without acute sleep deprivation.
ShiftWell uses this for
5 transition protocols — pre-shift, post-shift, rotating, recovery, and off-sequence shift types
Paper 03
Caffeine Half-Life and Sleep Architecture
Drake et al., 2013 · Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine
Caffeine consumed 6 hours before sleep significantly reduces sleep time by more than 1 hour. With a plasma half-life of approximately 5–7 hours, caffeine timing relative to the planned sleep window is critical — not just consumption amount.
ShiftWell uses this for
Caffeine cutoff calculator — your personalized last-cup time, recalculated for each shift
Paper 04
Time-Restricted Eating and Circadian Health
Manoogian et al., 2022 · Cell Metabolism
Aligning the eating window with the active circadian phase reduces metabolic disruption in shift workers. Even a 10-hour eating window, properly timed, can mitigate the elevated cardiometabolic risk associated with nocturnal shift work.
ShiftWell uses this for
Meal timing plan — eating window anchored to your circadian phase, not the clock on the wall
Paper 05
AHA Scientific Statement: Circadian Rhythms and Cardiovascular Health
American Heart Association, 2025
The AHA formally endorsed circadian-aligned interventions for shift workers, citing robust evidence that misalignment between behavioral and biological circadian rhythms increases cardiovascular disease risk — and that structured realignment protocols reduce it.
ShiftWell uses this for
Clinical endorsement baseline — the entire ShiftWell protocol maps to AHA-recommended interventions
