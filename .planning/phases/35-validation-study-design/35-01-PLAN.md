Write a full observational cohort study protocol following STROBE (Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) guidelines.

**Title:** "ShiftWell Circadian Sleep Optimization for Shift Workers: A Prospective Cohort Study"

**Background and Rationale:**
Shift work sleep disorder (SWSD) affects 10-38% of night shift workers (Drake et al. 2004). Existing interventions (pharmacological, behavioral) have modest effect sizes and poor adherence. Algorithm-guided personalized sleep scheduling has theoretical basis in the Two-Process Model (Borbely 1982) but has not been prospectively evaluated in a shift worker population with digital health tools. ShiftWell provides real-time algorithm-generated sleep windows synchronized to individual shift schedules — a novel intervention modality.

**Study Design:**
Prospective observational cohort study.
NOT a randomized controlled trial (RCT requires IRB with higher scrutiny, cannot randomize to "no sleep guidance" without ethical concerns).
Design choice: Pre-post design within subjects, 90-day observation period.
Why observational: Ethical considerations — denying algorithm access to control group raises concerns in fatigued healthcare workers. Observational data can still establish dose-response (adherence level → outcome improvement).

**Population:**

Inclusion criteria:
1. Age 18-65
2. Employed in shift work (nursing, EMS, police, fire, corrections, transportation, manufacturing)
3. Works rotating or night shifts (≥2 nights/week average over prior 3 months)
4. Owns iPhone with iOS 16.0+ (App Store access)
5. Willing to use ShiftWell for 90 consecutive days
6. Able to provide informed consent in English or Spanish

Exclusion criteria:
1. Diagnosed obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) currently untreated — confounds sleep quality measurement
2. Narcolepsy or other primary sleep disorder diagnosis
3. Pregnant or postpartum < 6 months (circadian rhythm physiology altered)
4. Currently using prescription sleep medications (benzodiazepines, z-drugs, orexin antagonists)
5. Plans to change shift type during study period (prevents clean pre-post comparison)

**Sample Size:**
Target: 100-150 participants (see STATISTICAL-ANALYSIS-PLAN.md for power calculation).
Anticipated dropout: 20% over 90 days → enroll 125 for 100 completers.

**Recruitment:**
Primary: In-app recruitment banner (consenting ShiftWell users)
Secondary: Social media (ShiftWell accounts targeting nursing communities)
Tertiary: Hospital nursing leadership (email to VP Nursing contacts)
Incentive: $25 Amazon gift card at 90-day completion (modest, does not introduce selection bias)

**Primary Outcome:**
Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) total score change from baseline to 90-day follow-up.
PSQI: validated 19-item instrument, score 0-21, higher = worse sleep quality.
Clinically significant change: ≥3-point reduction (established PSQI MCID for clinical interventions).
Administered: At enrollment (baseline), Day 30, Day 60, Day 90.

**Secondary Outcomes:**
1. Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) change — daytime sleepiness
2. Shift Work Sleep Disorder Diagnostic Questionnaire (SWQ) — SWSD symptom severity
3. ShiftWell adherence rate — % of nights where actual sleep was within 30 min of planned window
4. Recovery score trajectory — mean recovery score at weeks 1, 4, 8, 12
5. Sleep debt balance — mean debt at weeks 1, 4, 8, 12
6. Self-reported fatigue-related incidents at work (single item: "In the past 30 days, did fatigue affect your work performance?")

**Data Collection:**
Baseline: Online survey (REDCap or Typeform) collecting PSQI, ESS, SWQ, demographics, shift schedule
In-app: Continuous collection of adherence, recovery score, debt balance (automated, no burden)
Follow-up: Same survey at days 30, 60, 90 (automated push notification from app + email reminder)

**Timeline:**
Month 1: IRB submission + protocol finalization
Months 2-3: Participant recruitment and enrollment
Months 2-7: Active data collection (90-day observation period per participant, staggered enrollment acceptable)
Month 8: Data cleaning and analysis
Month 9-10: Manuscript writing
Month 11: Journal submission target

**IRB Pathway Analysis:**
Classification: Likely EXEMPT under 45 CFR 46.104(d)(2) (research involving educational tests, surveys, interviews, or observation of public behavior) because:
- Survey-only data collection (PSQI, ESS are validated questionnaires)
- No interaction beyond normal app use
- No identifiable PHI collected in research dataset

Conditional on EXEMPT: Submit to a university-affiliated IRB (Sim's medical school connection, or University of South Florida as Tampa institution) for determination.
If EXPEDITED required: Pathway D2 or D3 (minimal risk survey research).
Key risk: If HealthKit sleep data is deemed PHI in research context, may require EXPEDITED rather than EXEMPT.

**Authorship and Publication Target:**
Target journals (in priority order):
1. Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine (JCSM) — AASM flagship, high relevance
2. Sleep Health — broad audience, open access option
3. JMIR mHealth and uHealth — digital health focus, faster review

Authorship: Sim (Dr. Gursimran Singh) as first author. Include a sleep medicine collaborator as senior author for credibility (identify: AASM member, preferably with shift work research background).

**Ethics Statement:**
Informed consent obtained from all participants. Data de-identified before analysis. No compensation exceeding $25. Study registered on ClinicalTrials.gov before enrollment begins (NCT registration, required by most journals).

  <name>Task 2: Statistical analysis plan with power calculation (RES-20)</name>
  <files>.planning/phases/35-validation-study-design/STATISTICAL-ANALYSIS-PLAN.md</files>
  <action>
Write the pre-specified statistical analysis plan. All analyses defined BEFORE data collection to prevent HARKing (Hypothesizing After Results are Known).

**Section 1 — Power Calculation (Primary Outcome: PSQI change)**

Parameters:
- Effect size: Expecting moderate effect (Cohen's d = 0.5) based on:
  - Cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I): d = 0.78 (Trauer et al. 2015)
  - Digital health sleep interventions (less intensive): d = 0.35-0.55 (Scott et al. 2021 meta-analysis)
  - Conservative assumption: d = 0.5 (between CBT-I and passive digital health)
- Alpha: 0.05 (two-tailed)
- Power: 0.80 (standard, though 0.90 preferred — calculate both)
- Test: Paired t-test (within-subjects pre-post design)

Calculation:
- N required for 80% power: paired t-test, d=0.5, alpha=0.05 → n = 34 per pair
- N required for 90% power: n = 46
- With 20% dropout: enroll n = 42 (80% power) or n = 58 (90% power)
- ROADMAP target of 100+ participants provides ~95%+ power → well-powered study
- Secondary justification for n=100: enables subgroup analyses (night vs rotating shift) with adequate power

**Section 2 — Primary Analysis**

Test: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank if normality assumption violated — check with Shapiro-Wilk).
Outcome: PSQI total score at 90-day follow-up minus PSQI total score at baseline.
Hypothesis: H0: mean PSQI change = 0. H1: mean PSQI change < 0 (one-tailed for directional hypothesis).
Note: Pre-specify one-tailed vs two-tailed before seeing data. Recommend two-tailed for conservative publication standard.

Reporting: Mean change, 95% CI, p-value, Cohen's d with 95% CI, number needed to treat (NNT) at MCID threshold (≥3 points).

**Section 3 — Secondary Analyses**

3a. Dose-response analysis (adherence level as predictor):
- Divide participants into tertiles by adherence rate (low <40%, medium 40-70%, high >70%)
- ANCOVA with 90-day PSQI as outcome, baseline PSQI as covariate, adherence tertile as predictor
- Expected finding: higher adherence → greater PSQI improvement

3b. Subgroup analysis (shift type):
- Compare PSQI change: night-only vs rotating shift workers
- Two-sample t-test; adjust alpha for multiple comparisons (Bonferroni: 0.05/3 = 0.017)
- Caution: subgroup analyses hypothesis-generating only, not confirmatory

3c. Recovery score trajectory:
- Linear mixed effects model with time (weeks 1, 4, 8, 12) as within-subject factor
- Test: significant improvement in recovery score over time
- Report: slope coefficient with 95% CI

3d. ESS and SWQ secondary outcomes:
- Same paired t-test approach as primary
- Bonferroni correction for multiple secondary outcomes: alpha = 0.05/2 = 0.025

**Section 4 — Missing Data Strategy**

Primary analysis: complete case analysis (participants with both baseline and 90-day PSQI).
Sensitivity analysis: multiple imputation (m=20 imputations) using baseline characteristics + intermediate timepoints.
Dropout analysis: compare baseline characteristics of completers vs non-completers (t-test for continuous, chi-square for categorical). If systematic dropout bias found, report in limitations.

**Section 5 — Adherence Analysis**

Define adherence: "adherent" = night with actual sleep start within 30 min of planned sleep window.
Weekly adherence rate: adherent_nights / total_nights per week.
Primary adherence question: Does adherence rate improve over the 90-day period? Repeated measures ANOVA over weeks 1-12.

**Section 6 — Pre-Specified Subgroup Hypotheses**
(Written before data collection — these are a priori, not post-hoc)

Hypothesis A: Night shift workers will show greater PSQI improvement than rotating shift workers (night shift benefit more from consistent scheduling).
Hypothesis B: Participants with baseline PSQI ≥10 (moderate-severe sleep disturbance) will show greater absolute improvement than those with PSQI <10 (floor effect in mild cases).
Hypothesis C: Participants with >70% adherence will achieve mean PSQI change ≥3 points (clinically significant threshold).

**Section 7 — Reporting Standards**

Follow STROBE checklist for observational studies.
Report: CONSORT-style participant flow diagram (enrolled → completed → analyzed).
All p-values exact (e.g., p=0.023, not p<0.05).
Effect sizes with confidence intervals for all primary and secondary outcomes.
Software: R (preferred) or Python (scipy.stats, statsmodels). Report R version and packages used.
