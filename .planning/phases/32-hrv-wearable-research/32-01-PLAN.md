**HRV-LITERATURE-REVIEW.md:**

Section 1 — HRV Fundamentals
HRV (Heart Rate Variability) = variation in time intervals between heartbeats (RR intervals). Primary metric: RMSSD (root mean square of successive differences) — sensitive to parasympathetic tone, most relevant for recovery monitoring.

Key mechanisms:
- High HRV = strong parasympathetic (vagal) tone → good recovery, low stress
- Low HRV = sympathetic dominance → poor recovery, physiological stress, sleep deprivation
- Nocturnal HRV is highest-quality measurement window (sleep suppresses stress response)

Section 2 — HRV as Recovery Proxy (cite all sources):
1. Shaffer and Ginsberg 2017 (Front. Public Health) — HRV overview: RMSSD as optimal short-term metric
2. Plews et al. 2012 (Int J Sports Physiol Perform) — HRV-guided training vs fixed programs: HRV-guided superior
3. Bellenger et al. 2016 (Sports Medicine) — HRV for athlete monitoring: RMSSD 7-day rolling mean most stable
4. Buchheit 2014 (Front Physiol) — Monitoring training status with HRV: morning measurement protocol
5. Flatt and Esco 2015 (J Strength Cond Res) — 7-day HRV rolling average vs daily: rolling mean reduces noise
6. Kiviniemi et al. 2007 (Eur J Sport Sci) — HRV-guided vs fixed training: HRV group 30% greater VO2max gain
7. Stanley et al. 2013 (Int J Sports Physiol Perform) — Sleep and HRV: HRV suppressed by sleep deprivation
8. Thosar et al. 2018 (Chronobiol Int) — Circadian rhythm of HRV: nadir at 6am, peak at midnight
9. Tobaldini et al. 2013 (Eur Heart J) — Sleep and ANS: slow-wave sleep drives parasympathetic HRV peak
10. Bonnet and Arand 1998 (Sleep) — Hyperarousal and low nocturnal HRV in insomnia patients
11. Viola et al. 2007 (Physiol Behav) — Shift work and HRV: night shift workers show blunted nocturnal HRV
12. de Zambotti et al. 2019 (Sleep Med Rev) — Consumer wearable HRV accuracy review
13. Hernando et al. 2018 (Biomed Signal Process) — Short-term HRV during sleep stages: RMSSD highest in N3
14. Natale et al. 2021 (Chronobiol Int) — Chronotype and HRV: evening types show later HRV peak
15. Sammito and Bockelmann 2016 (EJPC) — HRV biofeedback for occupational stress

Section 3 — HRV and Shift Work (critical for ShiftWell context)
Viola et al. 2007 and subsequent replication studies show night shift workers have:
- Lower 24-hour HRV vs day workers matched for age/BMI
- Blunted nocturnal RMSSD peak (circadian HRV rhythm disrupted)
- Recovery of HRV after transition to days takes 3-7 days

ShiftWell implication: Low HRV during transition periods is EXPECTED and NORMAL — algorithm must not penalize users during known transition periods (Phase 9 protocols active).

Section 4 — Practical Thresholds
- RMSSD normal range for adults: 19-75 ms (wide inter-individual variation — population norms less useful than personal baseline)
- Critical insight: % change from personal baseline is more meaningful than absolute value
- Algorithm recommendation: calculate user's rolling 30-day RMSSD baseline, use % deviation as recovery signal

**WEARABLE-ACCURACY-ASSESSMENT.md:**

Section 1 — Apple Watch HRV Measurement
Apple Watch measures HRV using photoplethysmography (PPG) — green LED optical sensor on wrist. Derives SDNN (not RMSSD) in background, and RMSSD during Breathe app/meditation sessions only.

Key limitation: PPG-based HRV is less accurate than ECG-based HRV, especially during movement.
Best window: During sleep (low movement artifact). Background HRV readings during sleep are most reliable.

Section 2 — Accuracy vs Gold Standard
Studies comparing Apple Watch to ECG/PSG:
- Hernando et al. 2018: PPG-derived HRV within 5-10% of ECG-derived RMSSD during rest
- de Zambotti et al. 2019: Consumer wearables (including Apple Watch) show MAE of 3-8 ms for RMSSD at rest; degrades to 15-25 ms during light activity
- Natale et al. 2021: Apple Watch sleep HRV shows r=0.73 correlation with PSG-derived HRV (decent but not clinical grade)
- Accuracy factors: skin tone, tattoos, body hair on wrist all reduce PPG signal quality

Section 3 — Sleep Stage Detection Accuracy
Apple Watch sleep stage detection (watchOS 9+):
- Overall accuracy vs PSG: 78-82% (Smith et al. 2023 vs older wearables at 65-70%)
- Wake detection: 91% sensitivity
- REM detection: 68% sensitivity
- N3 (deep sleep) detection: 62% sensitivity
PSG remains gold standard (95%+ accuracy). Apple Watch is "good enough" for trend analysis, not clinical diagnosis.

Section 4 — Confidence Weight Recommendation
Given known accuracy limitations:
- Use Apple Watch HRV as a CONTRIBUTING signal (not primary signal) in recovery score
- Suggested weight: 25-35% of recovery score when available, 0% when unavailable (graceful fallback)
- Calibration: Require 14 days of baseline before HRV contributes to score (establish personal mean)
- Flag: Tag recovery scores that include HRV vs those that don't (transparency for user)

Section 5 — Processing Recommendation: Batch vs Real-Time
Real-time: Low battery impact but high noise (movement artifacts). Not recommended.
Batch (nightly): After user wakes, read last 6 hours of HRV data from HealthKit. Average overnight RMSSD. Compare to 30-day rolling mean. Apply to recovery score calculation.
Recommendation: Nightly batch processing via HealthKit background delivery. Read in morning trigger (existing AppState foreground event).

Section 1 — Current Recovery Score Architecture (from existing code)
The recovery score currently uses:
- Adherence history (% of last 14 nights where user followed sleep window)
- Sleep debt balance (total debt minutes, normalized)
- Shift transition factor (whether user is in a circadian protocol period)

The score is a weighted sum, normalized 0-100.

Section 2 — Adding HRV as a Signal

**Inputs:**
- `overnightRMSSD`: average RMSSD (ms) from last sleep session (from HealthKit HRV samples)
- `personalRMSSDBaseline`: 30-day rolling mean RMSSD (established after 14+ nights)
- `hrv_available`: boolean (user has Apple Watch + granted HealthKit HRV permissions)
- `baselineDaysAvailable`: integer (how many nights of HRV data exist)

**HRV Signal Calculation:**
```
// Only compute HRV signal when baseline is established
if (baselineDaysAvailable < 14 || !hrv_available) {
  hrv_signal = null;  // do not contribute to score
}

// Percent deviation from personal baseline
hrv_deviation = (overnightRMSSD - personalRMSSDBaseline) / personalRMSSDBaseline

// Map to 0-100 scale
// +30% above baseline → 100
// At baseline → 70
// -30% below baseline → 0
hrv_score = clamp(70 + (hrv_deviation / 0.30) * 30, 0, 100)
```

Rationale for 70 at baseline: HRV at personal baseline means "normal recovery" — not penalizing, not rewarding. Positive deviation suggests better-than-usual recovery.

**Section 3 — Updated Recovery Score Formula:**
```
// Component weights — must sum to 1.0
const WEIGHTS = {
  adherence:   hrv_available && baselineDaysAvailable >= 14 ? 0.40 : 0.55,
  debt:        hrv_available && baselineDaysAvailable >= 14 ? 0.30 : 0.45,
  hrv:         hrv_available && baselineDaysAvailable >= 14 ? 0.25 : 0.00,
  transition:  0.05,  // constant — circadian protocol penalty
};

recovery_score = (
  adherence_score * WEIGHTS.adherence +
  debt_score      * WEIGHTS.debt      +
  (hrv_score ?? 0) * WEIGHTS.hrv      +
  transition_score * WEIGHTS.transition
);

recovery_score = clamp(Math.round(recovery_score), 0, 100);
```

**Section 4 — Transition Period Handling:**
When user is in a circadian transition protocol (Phase 9 protocols active):
- HRV suppression is EXPECTED during transitions (Viola et al. 2007)
- Do NOT penalize: if `inCircadianTransition === true`, exclude HRV signal from score regardless of HRV_available
- Add flag: `hrv_suppressed_due_to_transition: boolean` in score metadata for transparency UI

**Section 5 — Baseline Calibration Protocol:**
Day 1-13: HRV data collected but NOT used in score. User sees "Calibrating HRV (Day X/14)" in recovery score detail view.
Day 14+: HRV contributes to score. User notification: "Your Apple Watch HRV is now improving your recovery score accuracy."
Baseline update: Rolling 30-day mean (new night replaces oldest night once 30 nights available).
Outlier rejection: Nights where `baseQuality < 0.5` (HealthKit signal quality flag) excluded from baseline.

**Section 6 — HealthKit Queries for Phase 33:**

```swift
// Query type: HKQuantityType for HKQuantityTypeIdentifierHeartRateVariabilitySDNN
// Time range: startDate = lastSleepWindowStart, endDate = lastSleepWindowEnd

```

Note: Apple Watch provides SDNN-based HRV samples in HealthKit (`HKQuantityTypeIdentifierHeartRateVariabilitySDNN`). SDNN and RMSSD are correlated (r ≈ 0.85-0.92) but not identical. Use SDNN as primary metric with same algorithm — RMSSD literature values apply approximately.
