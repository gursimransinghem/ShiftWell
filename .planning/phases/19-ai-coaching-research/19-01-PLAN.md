    **Section 1: Literature Review (10+ sources)**
    Synthesize findings from:
    - Luxton et al. 2016 — AI applications in mental health and behavioral coaching
    - Torous et al. 2021 — Digital mental health guidelines, LLM safety in clinical-adjacent contexts
    - FDA Digital Health Guidance 2023 — Software as Medical Device (SaMD) exemptions for wellness apps
    - Scholten et al. 2023 — Conversational agents in health promotion
    - Naslund et al. 2020 — Social media and digital health coaching outcomes
    - Martinez-Miranda & Aldea 2005 — Emotional intelligence in health coaching agents
    - Fitzpatrick et al. 2017 — Delivering cognitive behaviour therapy through chatbot (Woebot study)
    - Inkster et al. 2018 — AI-powered chatbot in mental wellness support
    - Prochaska et al. 2021 — Technology in health behavior change
    - Amagai et al. 2022 — Challenges and barriers to conversational AI in healthcare

    For each source, extract: claim, relevance to ShiftWell, implication for prompt design.

    **Section 2: Prompt Architecture**
    Define the system prompt structure for Claude in ShiftWell:
    - Role definition: "You are a sleep coach specializing in shift work circadian rhythm optimization. You are not a physician."
    - Context injection schema: what user data fields are passed (adherence%, sleep debt hours, shift schedule summary, discrepancy trends)
    - Response format constraints: max 150 words, exactly one recommendation, bullet points allowed, no numbered clinical steps
    - Temperature and model selection rationale (claude-3-5-haiku for brief generation — cost and latency tradeoffs)

    **Section 3: Tone Guidelines**
    Coach voice vs clinician voice — concrete examples:

    | Coach (allowed) | Clinician (prohibited) |
    |-----------------|------------------------|
    | "Your sleep timing has shifted later this week" | "You show signs of delayed sleep phase disorder" |
    | "Try moving bedtime 30 min earlier tonight" | "Prescribe melatonin 0.5mg at 9pm" |
    | "Your debt is trending up — rest is a priority" | "You are at risk for metabolic syndrome" |

    **Section 4: Content Boundaries**
    Explicit list of allowed and prohibited content domains. Allowed: sleep timing recommendations, schedule awareness, adherence encouragement, general sleep hygiene. Prohibited: diagnosis, symptom interpretation, medication, emergency guidance, mental health diagnosis.

    **Section 5: Failure Mode Analysis**
    Common LLM failure modes in health contexts: jailbreaks, prompt injection, hallucinated citations, emotional crisis handling. Mitigation strategy for each.

    Update `docs/research/SLEEP-SCIENCE-DATABASE.md` to append the 10+ new citations under a new section "AI Coaching & Digital Health".

    Create `.planning/phases/19-ai-coaching-research/SAFETY-GUARDRAILS.md`:

    **Section 1: Prohibited Output Categories**
    For each category, provide: definition, example of prohibited output, detection heuristic.

    Categories:
    1. Medical diagnosis — naming conditions, syndromes, disorders ("you have SWSD")
    2. Medication guidance — any specific drug, supplement dosage ("take melatonin X mg")
    3. Emergency clinical guidance — anything that implies urgent medical need
    4. Mental health assessment — depression, anxiety, burnout diagnosis or screening
    5. Prognosis — predicting health outcomes ("this pattern will cause X")
    6. Symptom interpretation — mapping symptoms to conditions
    7. Caloric/nutritional prescriptions — specific dietary targets beyond general timing
    8. Legal or employment advice — FMLA, ADA accommodation guidance

    **Section 2: Required Disclaimers**
    When Claude output must include a disclaimer (e.g., any output that touches on health impact), exact disclaimer text to inject: "This is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice."

    **Section 3: Crisis Detection Protocol**
    If user data or context suggests extreme sleep deprivation (>72h no sleep logged), suicidal ideation keywords in future feedback, or extreme distress signals — Claude must respond with emergency resources only, not coaching content.

    **Section 4: Prompt Injection Defenses**
    System prompt hardening: instructions that cannot be overridden by user content. Template showing how to structure the system prompt to resist injection.

    **Section 5: Content Review Process**
    How to update guardrails over time: monthly review cadence, trigger conditions for immediate update, version control for prompt versions.

    - Crisis indicators: extreme deprivation patterns, user self-report of distress (3+ cases)
