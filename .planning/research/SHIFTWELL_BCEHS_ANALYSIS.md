## 1. What Exactly Is This App?

### Overview
ShiftWell is a **wellness and shift-management mobile app** developed by the **BC Emergency Health Services (BCEHS) Critical Incident Stress program**. It is designed specifically for BCEHS paramedics and dispatchers.

### Feature List (Confirmed)
- **Shift schedule management** — manage complex shift schedules, includes a "platoon calendar"
- **Self-care goal tracking** — build healthy habits with self-care goals
- **Educational content** — access articles and videos created specifically for BCEHS staff
- **Support access** — find both urgent and non-urgent mental health/wellness support (including crisis line: 1-855-969-4321)
- **COVID-19 resources** — dedicated button for COVID-19 best practices (added during pandemic)
- **App tutorial** — onboarding walkthrough
- **Security updates** — data safety features

### What It Is NOT
- It is NOT a general consumer wellness app
- It is NOT a sleep science or circadian rhythm app
- It is NOT available to the general public (requires BCEHS account verification)
- It is NOT a scheduling/rostering tool for managers — it is a personal shift calendar + wellness hybrid

### App Category
Listed as **Health & Fitness** on both app stores.

### Target Audience
Exclusively BCEHS employees: paramedics, dispatchers, and patient transfer staff in British Columbia, Canada.

## 2. Who Made It?

### BC Emergency Health Services (BCEHS)
- **Type:** Government/public health agency — NOT a private company
- **Parent org:** Provincial Health Services Authority (PHSA), which is a publicly funded health authority under the Province of British Columbia
- **PHSA is unique in Canada** as the only health authority with a province-wide mandate for specialized health services
- **Size:** 4,000+ employees (paramedics, dispatchers, managers, physicians, admin)
- **Infrastructure:** 184 ambulance stations across British Columbia
- **Services:** Pre-hospital emergency care, inter-facility patient transfers, BC Ambulance Service, BC Patient Transfer Services, Community Paramedicine program
- **Website:** [bcehs.ca](https://www.bcehs.ca/)
- **LinkedIn:** [BC Emergency Health Services](https://ca.linkedin.com/company/bc-emergency-health-services)

### Development
- Developed under the **BCEHS Critical Incident Stress Management (CISM) program**
- The CISM program provides peer support and access to clinical trauma psychologists
- The program received provincial recognition from health employers
- **Developer/contractor unknown** — no public information on who actually coded the app (could be in-house or contracted out)
- BCEHS also has a separate app called "Handbook" (App Store ID: 1269664271) and a "BCEHS" app (App Store ID: 6475810027), suggesting some internal app development capability
- **App package ID:** `ca.bcehs.shiftwell` (Android) — the `ca.bcehs` prefix is their developer namespace

## 7. Legal Risk Assessment

### Risk Matrix

| Factor | Risk Level | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| BCEHS enforcing trademark | **VERY LOW** | Government agency, internal tool, no trademark filings found, app appears abandoned since 2021 |
| Shiftwell.ai enforcing | **MODERATE** | Active US startup with commercial product, named founders, possible common law rights. Different product category (HR/coaching vs. health/sleep) but closest in "wellness" overlap |
| Shiftwell.io enforcing | **LOW-MODERATE** | Scheduling SaaS — different product category but "shift" + "well" in scheduling space has overlap |
| Shiftwell.co enforcing | **VERY LOW** | Tiny prenatal fitness business in Vermont |
| App Store confusion | **LOW** | BCEHS app requires account verification, is Canada-only functionally, and appears unmaintained |
| Consumer confusion | **LOW** | Our product (shift worker health/sleep app) is different from all existing ShiftWell products |

### Detailed Analysis

**Arguments FOR using "ShiftWell":**
1. No found trademark registrations (USPTO or CIPO) for "ShiftWell"
2. The BCEHS app is a niche internal government tool with ~hundreds of users, not a commercial product
3. The BCEHS app appears abandoned (last update 2021)
4. Different product categories: our app is a circadian/sleep science app for shift workers; theirs is a CISM wellness tool for paramedics
5. Different geographies: BCEHS is Canada-only; our app targets US market
6. BCEHS is a government agency unlikely to aggressively enforce IP on an abandoned internal tool
7. The name is already fragmented across 5+ unrelated entities worldwide

**Arguments AGAINST using "ShiftWell":**
1. **Shiftwell.ai is the biggest concern** — active US startup, commercial product, AI wellness coaching. Even without a trademark registration, they may have common law rights and the resources/motivation to enforce
2. The name is crowded — SEO will be diluted across multiple entities
3. App Store search for "ShiftWell" will return the BCEHS app alongside ours — potential user confusion
4. A trademark examiner might cite any of these existing uses during our application process
5. The "shift" + "well" combination is descriptive for shift worker wellness, making it harder to get strong trademark protection for anyone
6. Multiple existing entities means multiple potential opposers if we file for trademark

### Trademark Attorney Recommendation (Predicted)

A trademark attorney would **likely advise caution** but not an outright "no":

- **If filing in the US:** Moderate risk. Shiftwell.ai's common law use in "AI coaching/wellness" space could be cited. The descriptive nature of the mark weakens everyone's claim but also weakens ours.
- **If filing in Canada:** Higher risk specifically because BCEHS has clear prior use of the exact mark in a health/wellness mobile app context in Canada.
- **Overall:** The attorney would likely recommend:
  1. Conducting a formal comprehensive trademark search ($300-800)
  2. Considering a more distinctive name to avoid the crowded "ShiftWell" space entirely
  3. If proceeding, filing narrowly in specific classes (e.g., Class 9 for software, Class 44 for health services) and being prepared for office actions

### Bottom Line

**The BCEHS app itself is low risk. But the broader "ShiftWell" landscape — particularly Shiftwell.ai — creates meaningful naming friction.** The name is not unique to us, is descriptive, and is used by multiple entities across multiple countries. This creates both legal risk and brand differentiation challenges.

**Recommendation: Seriously consider alternative names.** The time and money spent defending or clearing "ShiftWell" may exceed the value of the name, especially when the name is descriptive enough that we cannot build strong brand moats around it.
